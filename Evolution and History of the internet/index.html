History of the Internet

The Internet during the cold war.

By (WWW Foundation, 2022)

During the cold war between the Soviet Union and the United States a race between counties began to increase their technological capabilities to gain an advantage in the war. Computers were extremely big and expensive and could not communicate over long distances. This was essential for researchers to share valuable information. Internet development began in the 1960s when the Advanced Research Projects Agency Network (ARPANET) was established. Their team designed packet switching technology which is a crucial element for the design and creation for Transmission Control Protocol (TCP) and the Internet Protocol (IP) in the 1970s.

Evolution of the Internet

By (Khan, 2023)

The internet has experienced many changes along the years to aid the development and accessibility of the platform. In the early phases of the internet known as the Syntactic Web which enabled very basic communication and information display which could only be seen by its visual presentation. As a new digital era took place the Semantic Web was born which used the Syntactic web as a building block to aid its advancements. Tim Berners-Lee, the creator of the World Wide Web envisioned the Semantic web as an advanced user experience were machines understood the relations between data and not only display the information but process it using Technologies like RDF (Resource Description Framework). Further the Pragmatic web was born to understand user preferences, behaviours and intentions thus enabling personalized content delivery and interactions allowing a tailored experience to the user while being as efficient as possible. As technology progresses the complexity of these platforms will increase as well allowing a more efficient and tailored experience.

Important Dates during the Evolution of the Internet

Dates that played an important role in the Evolution of the Internet.

By (Khalid Hossen, 2019)

In the 1960's, the Advanced Research Project Agency (ARPA), was given a grant by U.S defence to start researching into developing a network. The goal was to develop protocols to allow computers to interconnect with each other regardless of the geographical area. The first successful network was created and was mainly used for academic and scientific research purposes on campuses. Down the road, a problem occurred- People using these different networks needed to connect to each other. In order to successfully interconnect, the networks needed to use the exact same protocols. These problems led to the invention of a global, unique- suit of protocols- called theTCP/IP suit. To take advantage of this huge network, and make use of it, many internet services have been engineered and created.
